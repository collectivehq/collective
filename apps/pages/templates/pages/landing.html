Why Collective exists

Built around clarity, stewardship, and decision memory.

Collective is for groups that need more than a comment thread. It is designed to help people understand the shape of a conversation, the rules around participation, and the outcome that came from it.

Clarity

The tree structure keeps big conversations readable by letting topics branch instead of pile up.

Stewardship

Roles, permissions, moderation, and participant management are core parts of the product, not add-ons.

Decision memory

Opinions, reactions, revisions, and resolution states help a group remember what changed and why.

Why it feels different

Better than choosing between chat chaos and flat forums.

What you can do

Features that support serious discussion without making the interface hard to use.

Tree-structured discussion

Break large topics into focused branches while keeping them connected to the whole.

Drafts and revision history

Write carefully, publish when ready, and keep a visible record of edits over time.

Explicit resolution states

Accept, reject, close, and reopen discussions so outcomes are easy to understand later.

Roles and permissions

Support facilitators, moderators, members, observers, and custom roles with clear capabilities.

Opinions and reactions

Show agreement, disagreement, and lightweight feedback without flattening everything into one vote.

Import from DOCX or Markdown

Start from real agendas, proposals, or working docs instead of rebuilding the structure by hand.
